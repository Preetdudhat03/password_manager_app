## The Problem
We all have too many accounts. Email, bank, social media, shopping... remembering a unique password for each one is impossible. So, many of us re-use the same password everywhere. This is dangerous‚Äîif hackers steal that one password, they can access everything you own.

## The Solution
Klypt is a digital safe on your phone. It remembers all your complex passwords for you. You only need to remember ONE strong "Master Password" to unlock the safe.

Most password apps store your data on their company servers (the "Cloud"). This is convenient, but it means you are trusting that company to keep your secrets safe. Klypt is different. It works entirely offline. Your passwords never leave your phone. It's like writing them in a physical notebook that only you can read, except much safer.

3Ô∏è‚É£ How Your Passwords Are Protected
================================================================================

Imagine a physical safe made of indestructible steel.

*   When you add a password to Klypt, the app puts it inside this safe and locks the door.
*   The "Lock" is a mathematical formula that scrambles your data into gibberish.
*   The "Key" is your Master Password.

Without the Key, the safe simply cannot be opened. Even if a supercomputer tried to guess the combination for a million years, it wouldn't succeed. This isn't just a marketing promise; it's based on the same standard security technology used by banks and governments worldwide.

4Ô∏è‚É£ What Is the Master Password?
================================================================================

The Master Password is the single most important thing in Klypt.

*   **It Unlocks Everything**: It is the only way to open your vault.
*   **We Do Not Know It**: The app does not send your Master Password to us. It does not save it anywhere permanent.
*   **If You Forget It, It Is Gone**: Because we don't know your password, we cannot reset it for you. There is no "Forgot Password" button that sends you an email. If you forget this password, your data is locked forever.

This sounds scary, but it is necessary for true security. If *we* could reset your password, then a hacker could trick us into resetting it for them. By removing that option, we remove that risk.

5Ô∏è‚É£ What Happens If Someone Steals Your Phone?
================================================================================

If a thief steals your phone, your passwords are still safe.

1.  **They Need Your Master Password**: Even if they can unlock your phone screen, they cannot open Klypt without your specific Master Password.
2.  **Biometrics Aren't Enough**: Even if they somehow trick the fingerprint scanner, the app requires the Master Password periodically and for sensitive actions (like exporting data).
3.  **The Data is Gibberish**: If they try to hack into the phone's storage files directly, all they will see is scrambled, nonsensical code. Without your password, that code cannot be turned back into readable text.

7Ô∏è‚É£ What Is an Encrypted Backup?
================================================================================

A Backup is a special file you can create inside the app. It makes a copy of all your passwords.

*   **It Is Also Locked**: This backup file is encrypted just like your vault. If you email it to yourself or save it on Google Drive, it is safe. Even if someone hacks your email and finds the file, they cannot open it without your Master Password.
*   **Your Responsibility**: You should create a backup regularly and save it somewhere SAFE (like a computer, a USB drive, or your personal cloud storage).
*   **Restoring**: If you get a new phone, you simply install Klypt, select "Restore from Backup", find that file, and enter your password. All your data will reappear instantly.
